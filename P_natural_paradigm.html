Natural Paradigms: Healing

2020

Overview

This project was an study of natural design paradigms. After observing the natural world around me, I was intruged by the ways in which natural heals, as well as the ways human have been able to aid in the healing process. From prosthetics to natural scabbing, there are many ways to heal. In contrast to the ever changing nature of life, I choose to model these processes on a casted rock, to symbolise the strength and resiliance it often takes to heal.

Materials

  • Alginate Molding Powder
  • Plaster
  • Wire
  • String
  • Hot Glue
  • Acrylic Paint
  • Spray Paint

Process

Using a rock I found while walking to observe the natural design paradigms around me, I began by creating a mold to pour plaster in with alginate molding powder. After creating my mold, I brainstormed the ways in which humans heal, as well as how plants and animals heal. Some were more obvious to represent, such as creating a splint out of wire to mimick a splint we might place in our bodies. However, some required a bit more abstaction, for example the yellow “prosthetic” I made for one of my rocks. Each one was cast and then broken after the fact, put back together in various ways just as living things are. I found it to be deeply satisfying to put the pieces back together, and was able to expand the limits of my creativity by finding new ways to do so.
