Spaces from Memory

2021

Overview

For this project, my overall focus was understanding the physical spaces I am in, and how that translates into both a three dimensional model and two dimensional drawings. I was prompted to “create an intimate architectural space from your childhood or a distant memory from memory”. This tested my ability to conceptualize a space from memory, strengthening my spatial memory and awareness.

Materials

  • Foam core
  • Pen
  • Glue
  • Paper

Process

In order to create this space from memory, I began first with sketchbook drawings. I had an estimate in my mind of what size various elements in the room were, and was able to use the spaces around me currently to better conceptualize this. After creating several versions in my sketchbook, I was able to settle on the measurements that worked best to keep the room proportional. Next, I moved on to creating a 3D model out of foam core. While I had done projects in foam core before, I had never done any where the cleanliness of my lines would be so important. For this reason, finding the best ways to cut and measure was a bit of a learning curve, but in the end I found my rhythm and was pleased with the way it turned out. Last, I made a drawing plan for the space. Using the same scale as the model, I created an isometric projection as well as a floor plan and two elevations. In the end, I was pleased with how my model and drawings came out, and feel they are an accurate representation of my childhood bedroom.
